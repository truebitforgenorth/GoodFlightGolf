🐺 Wolf Rules

Game Logic

How Payouts Work:

  • Team holes use the Base value that you set.
  • Lone, Dump, and Blind each use their own custom win and lose values.
  • When a hole has multiple winners, the payout is split evenly among those winners unless that scoring mode says otherwise.
  • When there is a solo winner, that player receives the full value for that scoring mode.
  • If carryover is enabled, tie points are added to the pot and paid out on a later winning hole.
  • This section explains how the app pays out points or money, not the full playing rules of Wolf.

How to Play Wolf Golf

Wolf is a golf game for four players where one golfer is the Wolf on each hole. After tee shots, the Wolf may choose a partner or play alone depending on the format your group uses.

Many groups also use variations like Lone Wolf, Blind Wolf, and Dump. This scorekeeper is built to help you keep those formats organized while tracking live payouts during the round.

Why use a Wolf Golf scorekeeper?

Wolf can get messy fast when you add points, dollar values, split payouts, and carryovers. A live scorekeeper helps your group avoid payout mistakes and makes it easier to focus on the game instead of paper math.

Wolf Golf FAQ

What is a Wolf Golf scorekeeper?

A Wolf Golf scorekeeper helps your group track players, teams, points, and payouts hole by hole during a Wolf round.

Can this page track Lone Wolf and Blind Wolf?

Yes. This page includes scoring support for Lone Wolf, Blind Wolf, Dump, team wins, and carryover setups.

Is Wolf a good golf betting game for foursomes?

Yes. Wolf is one of the most popular golf betting games for foursomes because it combines partner strategy, scoring pressure, and flexible payout options.
