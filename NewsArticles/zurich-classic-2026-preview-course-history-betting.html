Next Up on the PGA TOUR: Zurich Classic 2026 Preview, Past Winners, Course Notes and Betting Watch

3 min read | Posted 04/20/2026

The next PGA TOUR event is the Zurich Classic of New Orleans.
After Matt Fitzpatrick's playoff win at the RBC Heritage on Sunday, April 19, the TOUR heads straight to Louisiana for one of the most unusual stops on the schedule. The 2026 Zurich Classic of New Orleans runs from April 23-26 at TPC Louisiana in Avondale and remains the only official team event on the regular PGA TOUR calendar.

Why this week feels different.
This is not a standard four-day stroke-play event. The field features 80 two-man teams, with Four-ball played in Rounds 1 and 3 and Foursomes in Rounds 2 and 4. That means chemistry matters almost as much as talent. A team can make a pile of birdies in best-ball, then give some of it right back if alternate shot gets awkward in the wind, in the bunkers, or around the greens.

The headliners this time around.
Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry are one of the biggest stories in the field, while defending champions Ben Griffin and Andrew Novak return trying to repeat. Matt Fitzpatrick also stays in the spotlight after his RBC Heritage win, teaming again with his brother Alex Fitzpatrick. Other pairings drawing attention include Wyndham Clark and Taylor Moore, plus Aaron Rai and Sahith Theegala.

A little about the course.
TPC Louisiana is a Pete Dye design built with input from Steve Elkington and New Orleans native Kelly Gibson. The course stretches across more than 250 acres of wetlands near the Mississippi River delta and features over 100 bunkers, five ponds, cypress and oak framing, and a par-5 finishing hole that asks for nerve late. The public scorecard stretches to 7,400 yards from the tips, and the course tends to reward strong iron play, clean wedge work, and teams that can stay aggressive without getting sloppy.

What tends to win here.
The winning formula usually looks pretty simple: stack birdies in Four-ball, survive Foursomes, and avoid the kind of loose holes that turn alternate shot into damage control. The format makes this week especially fun because longshot pairings can hang around if they get hot at the same time. That also makes it one of the harder betting events on the schedule to price cleanly.

Early Betting Watch

Because this is a team event, the smarter way to frame the board is by teams, not individual players. As of Monday, April 20, 2026, at least one betting preview noted that many major books had still not fully posted a complete market-wide outright board yet after the Monday-finish RBC Heritage. Early previews still pointed toward the following teams as the names most likely to sit near the top tier once books settled in:

Our betting disclaimer, because yes, this part matters.
Gambling content is for entertainment and conversation only. Odds move, books disagree, and alternate shot can turn your favorite ticket into a bonfire in about nine minutes. We are not responsible if you lose money trying to get cute with a Thursday outright. Bet responsibly, check your local laws, and if you are not having fun anymore, step away.

The Zurich Classic is one of the weirdest, loosest, and most entertaining weeks on the PGA TOUR calendar. If you like team strategy, live dogs, and a leaderboard that can move fast, this is a fun one to watch.
