Matt Fitzpatrick Wins the 2026 RBC Heritage in a Playoff

2 min read | Posted 04/20/2026

Harbour Town delivered another Fitzpatrick finish.
Matt Fitzpatrick captured the RBC Heritage on Sunday, April 19, 2026, by beating Scottie Scheffler on the first playoff hole at Harbour Town Golf Links. The English star closed in 18-under 266 after rounds of 65-63-68-70, then produced the shot that mattered most when the tournament came down to one final swing.

A final-round lead that did not stay comfortable.
Fitzpatrick began Sunday with a three-shot edge and looked in control early, birdieing two of his first three holes to stretch the margin. Scheffler, though, kept hanging around. The world No. 1 turned late pressure into a real threat with birdies on the 15th and 16th, then watched Fitzpatrick make his only bogey of the day on the 72nd hole to force extra golf.

The winning moment matched the stage.
Back on the 18th in the playoff, Fitzpatrick answered with the kind of precise iron shot Harbour Town demands. PGA TOUR's recap credited him with a 4-iron to 13 feet into the wind, and he rolled in the birdie putt to secure his second tartan jacket. It was another RBC Heritage playoff win for Fitzpatrick, who also lifted the trophy here in 2023.

Why this win feels bigger than one week.
This was not just another solid finish on a strong schedule. It was Fitzpatrick's second PGA TOUR win of the 2026 season, following his Valspar Championship title in March, and his fourth PGA TOUR win overall. It also came against Scheffler in a signature event, which says plenty about the level of golf Fitzpatrick is playing right now. When the pressure rose and the crowd leaned toward the American favorite, Fitzpatrick still hit the shot of the tournament.

A tournament that clearly means something to him.
The RBC Heritage has long carried personal weight for Fitzpatrick, who has spoken about family trips to Hilton Head when he was younger. That history gave the victory a little more texture. Winning once at a place you love is special. Winning twice, including back-to-back playoff triumphs over major American stars at the same tournament, turns it into part of your story.
